The regulator's questions are not surprising. Lease length, ground rent, service charge schedule, staircasing rights, forfeiture triggers. They are the same questions every quarter, and they should be the same answers every quarter.

They are not.

The data the regulator wants lives in PDFs and people's heads. Lease documents, scanned management packs, email threads with solicitors, the heads of long-tenured staff who happen to remember the carve-outs. Pulling a clean answer out of it takes weeks, and the answer is rarely the same twice.

## Structured beats scanned

A clean compliance posture is not about how much paperwork you have. It is about how much of that paperwork has been turned into structured, queryable data — and how confident you are in each field.

That is what Stairpay Compliance does:

- **Extract.** Lease PDFs become structured records. Each field has a confidence score.
- **Monitor.** Gaps and inconsistencies surface before the regulator finds them.
- **Trigger.** Renewals, rent reviews, lease events — automated from the structured data, not chased on a calendar.

## What changes

The board paper writes itself. The regulator's request is a query, not a project. The team that used to spend a week reverse-engineering the portfolio spends that week on residents instead.
