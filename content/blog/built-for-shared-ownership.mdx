Most software in the housing sector wasn't built for housing. It was built for something else first — sales pipelines, property management, generic case work — and then bent into shape to fit. The result is the same story everywhere: teams working around the tools instead of with them.

Stairpay was built differently. Every workflow in the platform reflects how Shared Ownership actually works, not how a generic CRM thinks it should.

## Designed with Housing Associations from day one

We built Stairpay alongside some of the UK's largest Housing Associations. Every screen, every field, every state in the workflow came out of real cases — not a feature backlog. That means the product knows about nomination periods, S106 obligations, equity tracking, valuation timelines, and the regulatory questions that come with each.

Teams don't have to translate their work into the software's language. The software speaks theirs.

## The detail that matters

Shared Ownership is full of edges that generic systems flatten. Lease length and ground rent that need to be queryable, not buried in PDFs. Staircasing rights and forfeiture triggers that need to be tracked over time. Affordability rules that change by region and product. Resales that aren't quite sales and aren't quite lettings.

Stairpay handles those edges as first-class concepts, not free-text notes in a CRM record. That's what makes the difference between a tool that produces clean reports and one that produces a heroic intern.

## Why it matters now

The next decade of homeownership is going to bring new pathways: Private Shared Ownership, DIYSO, institutional models, hybrid tenure. They all share the same DNA — and they all need the same depth of understanding to be delivered well.

A platform that knows Shared Ownership inside out is the only kind that can grow with the market. That's the foundation we've built — and it's why Housing Associations choose Stairpay.
