Most Shared Ownership tools serve one side. They are HA tools, where the resident is a row in a database. Or they are resident tools, where the HA is whoever is on the other end of the email.

Stairpay is two-sided. The resident has an app. The HA has a workflow. They read and write to the same record.

## What two-sided actually means

It means a resident can instruct staircasing in the app, and the case opens in the HA's workflow with the affordability, the lease terms, and the document trail already attached. No re-keying. No exported CSV. No "can you forward me that email."

It means a HA can request a document, and the request lands in the resident's app — not in their inbox, not buried below an Argos delivery notification.

It means that when the regulator asks how many residents are at risk of staircasing this year, the answer is a query against a live, two-sided record. Not a project.

## Why nobody else does this

Building one side is hard. Building two and keeping them in sync is harder. Every other Shared Ownership tool we have looked at made a choice about which side to serve. We made the choice not to choose.

That is the moat. It is also the reason the platform compounds — every product gets stronger as the other side gets richer.
