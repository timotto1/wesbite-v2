A typical staircasing case takes a quarter of a year to close. Not because the legal work is hard, but because the case is run across an email thread, a Word template, a shared drive, and at least one spreadsheet that nobody trusts.

The resident waits. The solicitor chases. The valuer goes quiet. The HA carries the case-management load alone. And every party — solicitor, valuer, mortgage broker, HA team — works from a slightly different version of the same record.

## What we replaced it with

We started Stairpay with one principle: every party should be reading from the same case. Not their own copy of the case. The case.

When the resident instructs in-app, the case opens automatically. Affordability, lease terms, share value, and outstanding documents are all on file. The solicitor and valuer plug into the same record. Status updates land where everyone is already looking.

## The result

Six weeks instead of fourteen. Fewer dropped cases. Fewer "where are we with this one?" emails. And a record that the regulator can audit without a spreadsheet rebuild.

That's where Stairpay started. It's still where Stairpay is deepest.
